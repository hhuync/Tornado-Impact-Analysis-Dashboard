# Tornado Impact Analysis Dashboard

## Overview

This project analyzes tornado frequency, intensity, and associated impacts across the United States using interactive Power BI visualizations. The dashboard explores long-term tornado trends, compares tornado intensity levels, and examines how tornado activity relates to injuries, deaths, and damages over time.

The project was completed for Data 201: Data Visualization at Dickinson College in collaboration with a client scenario involving the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC).

---

## Objectives

* Analyze tornado frequency trends over time
* Compare tornado intensity levels using the Enhanced Fujita (EF) Scale
* Investigate relationships between tornado severity and human impact
* Identify geographic patterns among highly affected states
* Communicate weather risk insights through interactive visual storytelling

---

## Tools & Technologies

* Power BI
* DAX
* Data Cleaning & Transformation
* Interactive Data Visualization

---

## Dashboard Features

### Tornado Frequency vs Selected Impact

A combined visualization comparing tornado counts with selected impact metrics such as total injuries and deaths over time.

### Tornado Intensity vs Selected Impact

An analysis of how tornado intensity categories (EF0-EF5) relate to injuries and deaths, highlighting the disproportionate impact of stronger tornadoes.

### Tornado Frequency and Intensity Over Time

A dual-axis visualization examining trends in tornado occurrence alongside average tornado intensity.

### Tornado Frequency Across High-Risk States

A multi-line visualization comparing tornado activity across selected states with historically high tornado frequencies.

### Interactive Impact Filter

Users can dynamically switch between different impact metrics, including:

* Total Damage
* Total Deaths
* Total Injuries

---

## Key Insights

* Tornado frequency fluctuated significantly across the observed years.
* Higher EF-scale tornadoes were associated with substantially greater injuries and deaths.
* Certain states consistently experienced elevated tornado activity.
* Severe tornado events, while less frequent, contributed disproportionately to overall human impact.

---

## Project Goals

This project aimed to combine analytical reasoning with accessible visual communication to better understand patterns in severe weather events. Through interactive dashboards and comparative visualizations, the project demonstrates how data storytelling can support risk awareness and decision-making.
